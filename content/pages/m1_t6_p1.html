CP Transformer rectifier units

  • Most common type of power supply used for impressed current cathodic protection is a transformer rectifier units (also known as TR or TRU)
  • Converts the AC power supply voltage to the required output voltage and then converts it to DC
  • Ratings of the CP rectifier units used in PIL are
    • For Station CP - 50V/75A
    • For mainline CP – 25V/25A

Anode

  • Materials used for feeding the required current to the pipeline
  • For sacrificial system, generally used anodes are Zinc or Magnesium
  • For ICCP system the type of anodes used are Mixed Metal oxide and Hi - Silicon cast Chromium

Half Cell/Reference cell

  • Used for measuring the potential of a metal surface exposed to an electrolyte
  • Reference electrode used is saturated Cu/CuSO4 reference cell
  • Electrode is composed of a copper rod, immersed in a saturated solution of copper sulfate, held in a non-conducting cylinder with a porous plug at the bottom

Test Stations

  • Are junction boxes installed along the pipeline route at every 1km for measurement of PSP
  • Cable connection from pipeline underground is terminated inside the Test station for measurement of PSP
  • Different types of test stations are used based on installation location
